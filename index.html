Discover Options Close to Home

Athens

residential campus

Our residential campus is integrated into Uptown Athens (never call it downtown). Named one of the best college towns in the nation, Athens also boasts access to hiking, biking and more in the Southeast Ohio foothills.

More About Athens Visit Athens Campus

Athens is a small but eclectic city located among rolling hills, flowing rivers, a national forest and more. This is a place where city, campus, and community combine for an educational experience unlike any other.

The Alumni Gateway marks the entry from legendary Court Street in Athens to historic College Green.

Commencement ceremony honors and celebrates students as they graduate and officially earn their degrees.

Paw Print Park features shaded hammock space that can accommodate up to 56 hammocks, as well as space for student events.

There's a reason green is our main color; the most beautiful nature in Ohio is right at our doorstep.

Cleveland

extension campus

Embedded within Cleveland Clinic's South Pointe Hospital in Warrensville Heights, our Cleveland location exclusively serves students enrolled in our Heritage College of Osteopathic.

Our Cleveland students are part of a city known for history, culture, beautiful parks and a multitude of entertainment venues.

At the Heritage College, you'll find yourself immersed in a comprehensive, clinically oriented curriculum - one marked by intense experiential learning and a sharp focus on patient care.

Our Cleveland medical students get the opportunity to witness real-world applications of what they're learning.

Our Transformative Care Continuum is an accelerated pathway to family medicine, preparing students in just 6 years.

Dublin

extension campus

OHIO's Dublin Extension Campus serves as part of our three-campus medical school and home to professional graduate programs in business and public affairs.

OHIO's Dublin extension campus serves professionals in the Columbus market with professional programs including executive MBA and executive MPA degrees and multiple advanced degrees in health care.

Dublin is one of three locations for the Heritage College of Osteopathic Medicine, where we train physicians in partnership with OhioHealth.

Central Ohio high school students have the opportunity to earn exposure for healthcare careers at our annual Dublin-based Medical Academy.

OHIO's College of Health Sciences and Professions offers degrees DNP, CRNA, Physician Assistant and Athletic Training Programs at the Dublin campus.

Chillicothe

regional campus

Students on our Chillicothe campus earn career-aligned degrees in health, business, education, social work and more and go on to serve as leaders in their community.

The Chillicothe campus is a community cornerstone where leaders are built, and industry partners can connect with the talent they need.

Our Chillicothe graduates go on to serve as leaders in their community.

Chillicothe campus has a service area that encompasses several counties, and consists of 96 wooded acres which includes a disc golf course.

Our nursing students learn through a combination of classroom, laboratory, and supervised clinical instruction.

Eastern

regional campus

At the Ohio University Eastern campus, you'll find the close-knit sense of community of a small campus while earning an Ohio University degree in fields such as nursing, business and more.

OHIO's Eastern campus consists of 315 acres set in Ohio's beautiful rolling hills.

Our Eastern campus students get to experience the small-campus sense of community throughout their studies, going on to make a real impact on the surrounding area.

The size of the Eastern campus allows its students to build close relationships with each other and community leaders.

Ohio University's Eastern campus offers practical learning options, community engagement, and affordability, making it a strong choice for those looking to advance their education close to home.

Lancaster

regional campus

Located approximately 30 miles from Columbus, the Ohio University Lancaster campus provides access to affordable academic opportunities and workforce development training.

The Lancaster campus is located in beautiful Fairfield County, readily accessible from Columbus and nearby counties.

Lancaster campus students become part of a community, with friendly faculty and staff committed to their success.

Students from our Lancaster campus graduate with a high-quality and affordable educational experience, continuing their path toward a fulfilling career.

The workforce development opportunities on OHIO's Lancaster campus provide training programs for high-demand industries like manufacturing, skilled trades and health care.

Southern

regional campus

Located in Ironton along the Ohio River, Ohio University Southern prepares students for in-demand careers in the community, with more than half of the students at OHIO Southern studying nursing.

Ohio University Southern is located in the center of the Ironton-Portsmouth-Ashland-Huntington metropolitan area involving Ohio, Kentucky and West Virginia.

Ohio University Southern is among fewer than 300 universities in the country to have earned designation as an â€œOpportunity College and Universityâ€ in the recently released Carnegie Classifications.

OHIO Southern is a community cornerstone where leaders are built, and industry partners can connect with the talent they need. It's been recognized by Carnegie as a â€œSpecial Focusâ€ institution for its large percentage of graduates in nursing and health professions.

Southern campus features 30 associate and bachelor's degrees in technical and workforce areas as well as liberal arts. Our programs prepare students for careers in a variety of fields in business, education, equine studies, and more.

Zanesville

regional campus

Ohio University Zanesville is a leading producer of nurses and teachers for east central Ohio and offers a variety of in-demand degrees in communications, business, healthcare and recreation.

Ohio University Zanesville has a service area that encompasses several counties, consisting of 179 park acres set in the city of Zanesville.

Founded in 1946, the Zanesville campus is committed to supporting the needs of the surrounding region.

Ohio University Zanesville is among fewer than 300 universities in the country to have earned designation as an â€œOpportunity College and Universityâ€ in the recently released Carnegie Classifications.

All five of Ohio University's regional campuses (and the Athens residential campus) offer strong Nursing degree programs. Clinical experiences occur in hospitals and clinical agencies that are near each campus.
